FIGMA TIPS GENERATED BY GEMINI 
1. Onboarding & Auth 
Tip: "Value First, Data Second." Don't force a full sign-up before the user sees the value. Let them 
choose their exam target and take the Initial Level Assessment first. Once they see their "Estimated 
Score," they will be much more motivated to create an account to save that progress. 
2. Home (Daily Practice) 
Tip: "The Power of the Streak." To ensure daily retention, make the "Streak" (fire icon) the most 
prominent visual element on this screen. Use haptic feedback (vibrations) when a user completes 
their daily goal. The "Learning Path" should feel like a game—use bright colors for unlocked levels 
and grayscale for locked ones. 
3. Practice (Library) 
Tip: "Minimize Choice Paralysis." With 5 exams and dozens of sections, users can get 
overwhelmed. Implement a "Smart Filter" or a "Recommended for You" section at the top based 
on their weakest skill (e.g., "Your Speaking score is low—try this TOEFL Task 1 practice"). 
4. The Exercise Engine (The Player) 
Tip: "Focus Mode UI." When a user is inside an exercise, hide the main navigation (tabs). The UI 
must be "zero-distraction." For Reading tasks, implement a split-screen scroll where the text stays on 
the left and questions scroll on the right. This mimics the actual digital exam environment (like the 
real TOEFL iBT). 
5. AI Tools (The Hub) 
Tip: "Manage Latency with 'Thinking' States." AI generation can take 3–10 seconds. Never show a 
static loading spinner. Instead, use shimmer effects or "AI is analyzing your grammar..." text updates. 
For the Writing Corrector, use a "diff" view (Original vs. Corrected) so users can clearly see exactly 
what changed and why. 
6. Results & Analytics 
Tip: "Actionable Feedback Only." A score of "6.5" is useless without a "How to get to 7.0." Ensure 
every result screen ends with a single, high-value Next Step button (e.g., "Practice the Vocabulary 
used in this text"). Use Radar Charts to show skill balance—it’s the most intuitive way for students 
to see they are "Good at Reading, Bad at Speaking." 
7. Profile & Settings 
Tip: "The Achievement Gallery." People love collecting things. Make the Badge/Achievement 
gallery visually appealing. When a user reaches a milestone (e.g., "7-Day Streak"), allow them to 
share a generated "Success Card" to social media. This is free marketing for your app.
